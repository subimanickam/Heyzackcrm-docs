The Reports section is where you analyze the health and performance of your partner business. Every metric draws from live data in your account — installations you have scheduled and completed, revenue your handymen have generated, and feedback your customers have submitted. Use these reports to spot trends, identify gaps, and make decisions backed by data rather than guesswork.

## Navigating to Reports

Select **Reports** from the left sidebar to open the section. The page loads with the **Installations** tab active and the **Last 30 days** date range selected by default.

## Choosing a date range

The date range selector sits at the top of the Reports page and applies to all five report tabs. You can switch ranges at any time and each tab updates instantly.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Today">
    Shows data for the current calendar day only. Useful for monitoring same-day installation progress and activity.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Last 7 days">
    Shows a rolling 7-day window ending today. Good for spotting short-term trends in completion rates or customer feedback.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Last 30 days">
    The default view. Shows a rolling 30-day window and gives a reliable snapshot of your recent operational performance.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Last 90 days">
    A quarterly view that reveals seasonal patterns in installation volume and revenue.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Custom range">
    Opens a two-month calendar picker. Select a start date, then an end date. The range applies as soon as both dates are chosen. Use this for custom reporting periods such as a specific campaign, quarter, or contract period.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Report categories

The Reports page is organized into five tabs. Each tab focuses on a different dimension of your business.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Installations">
    Tracks every installation in your account — total count, completion rate, average job duration, and pending backlog. Includes daily trend charts and a breakdown by device type. See [Installation performance reports](/reports/installation-reports) for details.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Handyman Performance">
    Ranks each handyman by installations completed, star rating, average response time, number of certifications held, and revenue generated. Includes a skill-level radar chart across key categories such as smart locks, cameras, thermostats, and network setup.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Inventory">
    Shows product stock levels, order history, and device usage across your installations. Useful for planning upcoming orders and identifying which products see the highest demand.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Financial">
    Breaks down revenue by source — partner revenue, product sales, and service fees — alongside profit margins by category, payment collection aging, and a partner commission summary table. See [Financial reports](/reports/financial-reports) for details.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Customer Satisfaction">
    Displays your Net Promoter Score (NPS), average customer rating, survey response rate, and issue resolution rate. Includes NPS trend lines, satisfaction scores by installation type, and a weekly feedback sentiment breakdown. See [Financial reports: revenue, tier, and sales data](/reports/financial-reports) for context on how satisfaction scores relate to your partner standing.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Refreshing report data

Select the **Refresh** button in the top-right corner of the Reports page to reload the current tab with the latest data from your account. This is useful if you have recently completed installations or if you want to confirm that newly submitted customer feedback has been captured.

<Note>
  Data in all report tabs reflects your partner account only. You see metrics for your own installations, handymen, and customers — not data from other partners.
</Note>

## Exporting reports

Select the **Export** button to download report data. Exports are available in PDF, Excel, and CSV formats depending on the report type.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set your date range">
    Choose the date range that covers the period you want to export before clicking **Export**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Select the correct tab">
    Navigate to the report tab you want to export — for example, **Financial** or **Installations**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Export">
    Select **Export** in the top-right corner. The file downloads automatically to your browser's default download location.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Export financial data at the end of each month to maintain consistent records for accounting and partner reviews.
</Tip>
